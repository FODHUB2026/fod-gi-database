How This Works

This calculator sums all your diabetes-related monthly expenditures across four categories:

Monthly Total =
Medications (sum of all meds)
+ Testing (strips x cost/strip + CGM if used)
+ Supplies (insulin supplies + other)
+ Visits (visits/month x cost/visit)

Annual Projection = Monthly Total x 12

The doughnut chart shows the proportion of spending in each category - helping you identify where costs are concentrated and where cost-saving efforts will have the most impact.

Tips to Reduce Your Costs
  • Ask about generics: Generic metformin, glipizide, and other diabetes medications can cost 60-90% less than brand-name equivalents.
  • Bulk buying: Purchasing 90-day supplies of medications and test strips often carries a lower per-unit cost.
  • Manufacturer programs: Most insulin manufacturers offer patient assistance programs - Novo Nordisk, Sanofi, and Eli Lilly all have income-based programs.
  • Pharmacy price comparison: Prices can vary 2-4x between pharmacies for the same medication. GoodRx and similar tools help identify lowest local prices.
  • Optimize your insurance: Review your formulary annually - switching to a preferred tier medication can save significantly with the same therapeutic effect.
  • HSA/FSA accounts: All diabetes supplies qualify as medical expenses. Using pre-tax HSA or FSA funds effectively gives you a 20-35% discount depending on your tax bracket.
