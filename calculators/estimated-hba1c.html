How This Works

This calculator uses the DCCT (Diabetes Control and Complications Trial) formula, the most widely used equation for estimating HbA1c from average blood glucose:

HbA1c (%) = (Average Glucose mg/dL + 46.7) / 28.7

For mmol/L values, the input is first converted to mg/dL using: mmol/L x 18.018.

HbA1c reflects the average percentage of hemoglobin molecules in your red blood cells that have glucose attached. Because red blood cells live roughly 90 days, it provides a rolling 2-3 month picture of blood sugar control - making it the standard clinical marker for diabetes management.

Note: This is an estimated HbA1c. Lab-measured results may differ due to factors such as hemoglobin variants, recent illness, or measurement timing.
